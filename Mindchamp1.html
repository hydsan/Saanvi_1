Top Best Dog Breeds:

If you are wondering and looking for which dog breed is the best for you, you have come to the right place! These are some dog breeds which are fit for large families looking for a new member, or a protection against theifs and or just to play with. We recommend to adopt!

1. Labrador

The first breed is The sturdy, well-balanced Labrador Retriever can, depending on the gender, stand from 21.5 to 24.5 inches at the shoulder and weigh between 55 to 80 pounds.
The dense, hard coat comes in many colours such as yellow, black, and a luscious chocolate. The head is wide, the eyes glimmer with kindliness, and the thick, tapering 'otter tail' seems to be forever signaling the breed's innate eagerness. Labs are famously friendly. They are companionable housemates who bond with the whole family, and they socialize well with neighbor dogs and humans alike.
But don't mistake his easygoing personality for low energy: The Lab is an enthusiastic athlete that requires lots of exercise, like swimming and marathon games of fetch, to keep physically and mentally fit.

About:

  • Lifespan: 10 – 12 years (Adult)
  • Temperament: Outgoing, Even Tempered, Intelligent, Kind, Friendly, Agile, Gentle, Trusting
  • Colors: Black, Chocolate, Yellow
  • Weight:Male: 29–36 kg, Female: 25–32 kg
  • Hypoallergenic: No

2. Poodle

Many often discount the Poodle due to its fashionable posh pooch stereotype, but this couldn't be further from the truth. Originally bred as working dogs, poodles are super smart,
have plenty of energy for playing with children and are also generally kind and well-mannered when provided with enough entertainment.
They're also great for families with allergies as they're considered to be a hypoallergenic dog, given that they shed very little.

About:

  • Height: Toy Poodle: 24 – 28 cm, Miniature Poodle: 28 – 35 cm, Standard Poodle: 45 – 60 cm, Medium Poodle: 35 – 45 cm
  • Temperament: Intelligent, Alert, Active, Instinctual, Trainable, Faithful
  • Life span: 12 – 15 years
  • Hypoallergenic: Yes
  • Colors: Black, White, Apricot, Cream, Sable, Black & White, Silver, Red, Grey, Brown, Blue
  • Coat: Curly
  • Weight: Standard: 20–32 kg (44–71 lb); Medium: 9–13 kg (20–29 lb); Miniature: 4.5–7 kg (9.9–15.4 lb); Toy: 2–3 kg (4.4–6.6 lb)

3. Bernese Mountain Dog

Often known as 'gentle giants', Bernese Mountain dogs have a lovable, fun personality. When well socialised, they make one of the best dog breeds for kids as they're usually very patient and kind. They're particularly well suited to active households with older children who like to engage in outdoor activities
such as hiking, camping and exploring in nature. However, this family dog breed will not be suitable for very small children as they can easily knock them over and accidentally hurt them whilst playing.

About:

  • Life expectancy: 6 – 8 years
  • Hypoallergenic: No
  • Temperament: Affectionate, Intelligent, Loyal, Faithful
  • Weight: Male: 38–50 kg, Female: 36–48 kg
  • Origin: Switzerland
  • Color: Black tricolor

4. Beagle

Not only are Beagles considered to be one of the best dog breeds for families, but also one of the friendliest in general. With energetic and cheeky personalities,
they love their pack and enjoy nothing more than getting up to mischief with their smaller companions. And, despite their excitable nature they're also
generally very tolerant and calm with children.

About:

  • Life Expectancy:12-15 years
  • Temperament: Amiable, Curious, Even Tempered, Intelligent, MORE
  • Hypoallergenic: No
  • Height: Male: 36–41 cm, Female: 33–38 cm

5. Golden Retriever

Like a Labrador, the Golden Retriever is also incredibly popular with families, so much so that it's
depicted as 'the family dog' in many TV shows and films. With their kind temperaments and friendly attitudes, you'll soon see why this
is the case. They have bags of energy and adore playing with kids, and have even been known to follow them around, patiently waiting for the next opportunity to play (or cuddle)!

About:

  • Life span: 10 – 12 years
  • Temperament: Intelligent, Kind, Friendly, Reliable, Confident, Trustworthy
  • Colors: Dark Golden, Light Golden, Cream, Golden
  • Hypoallergenic: No

6. Cavalier King Charles Spaniel

Cavalier King Charles Spaniels are affectionate and loving dogs who are generally great with both children
and other pets. This small dog breed adores attention and is always up for a good play session with their family members. Additionally,
they're quite easy-going, laid-back dogs which are very forgiving with children. Plus,
it's thought to be very rare for a Cavalier King Charles Spaniel to show any kind of aggression.

About:

  • Temperament: Affectionate, Playful, Sociable, Fearless, Patient, Gentle, Graceful, Adaptable
  • Life expectancy: 9 – 14 years
  • Hypoallergenic: No
  • Origin: United Kingdom
  • Colour: Blenheim, black-and-tan, ruby or tri-colour
  • Weight: 5.4–8 kg (12–18 lb)

7. Staffordshire Bull Terrier

This one may surprise you, but Staffordshire Bull Terriers are actually thought to be one of the best dog breeds for
kids and have even earned themselves the name 'nanny dog'. When well socialised,
they possess sweet natures and absolutely love life, people and playing with their family.
Sadly, due to the negative stereotype the breed has, the Blue Cross alone takes in over 400 Staffordshire Bull Terriers a year, so if you're looking for
one of these wonderful dogs, we recommend checking at your local rescue centres.

  • Temperament: Affectionate, Intelligent, Bold, Loyal, Reliable, Fearless, Courageous
